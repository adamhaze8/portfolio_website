Magnus

During the summer of 2024, I had the priviledge of working alongside world-class roboticists at HEBI Robotics, where my main project was the creation of Magnus. Magnus is a novel mobile robot engineered to climb large ferromagnetic structures with versatility, operating autonomously or via remote control based on structural complexity. It features a 5-DOF arm, symmetric around the elbow/middle joint, and uses magnetic pads at both its base and end effector to adhere to surfaces.

Hardware Design

Magnetic Pads: Designed magnetic pads that could be mechanically toggled for both surface attachment and detachment. The pads needed to securely hold the robot in various kinematic and dynamic configurations while remaining light enough for the robot to lift its own weight. One major challenge was balancing the strength of magnetic adhesion with the need for a lightweight design.

Tether-Free Operation: Removed the need for a tether by adding a Raspberry Pi and battery, allowing the robot to operate without external connections.

Software Development

Manual Control: Implemented manual control and determined the appropriate spatial frames of reference for mapping joystick controls for the most intuitive user experience.

Stepping Algorithms: Experimented with various stepping algorithms in MATLAB for semi-manual or autonomous movement of the robot.

Autonomous Movement: Added robot autonomy, enabling Magnus to move to any 2D position on a planar surface.

Surface Transitions: Integrated automatic transitions between ground, wall, and ceiling surfaces, allowing the robot to navigate complex environments. To complete these transitions safely, the robot takes advantage of the torque sensing capabilites of HEBI actuators to feel and find the relative slope of perpendicular surfaces.


Wireless Control: In the final stage, ported the software to Python to enable wireless control of the robot.
