Motivation & Project Origin

Developing a dynamically balancing bipedal humanoid is notoriously one of the most punishing challenges in robotics. While industry leaders like Figure AI have raised over $1 billion to tackle these challenges and companies like Tesla commit massive capital expenditures toward humanoid development, my motivation was driven by a deep-seated obsession with the field and a desire to tackle the problem from first principles.

Rather than relying on expensive, off-the-shelf integrated actuators, this platform was intentionally engineered from scratch. By designing custom cycloidal transmissions and native impedance controllers from raw components, the objective was to maximize learning across the entire robotics stack—from machining raw aluminum to deploying complex reinforcement learning policies—embracing the immense difficulty of the process regardless of whether the final hardware walked or fell.

Project Objective

To design, manufacture, and program a 10-DOF bipedal humanoid capable of dynamic balancing and robust fall recovery. Originally scoped for a 0.8m to 1.3m form factor, the final platform was optimized at 1.1m and 22-25kg. A strict design constraint was enforced to engineer all mechanical and kinematic systems from scratch, limiting off-the-shelf sourcing exclusively to foundational electronic components such as raw brushless motors and microcontroller boards.

Technical Approach

  • Hardware & Modular Actuation: The physical architecture prioritizes strict modularity to support rapid iteration. The custom cycloidal actuators were built on a fully parametric CAD foundation, allowing multiple torque profiles (e.g., 16:1 and 20:1) to be easily generated as scaled versions of a single core design. To balance weight and structural integrity, the actuator housings are 3D printed in PETG, while the high-stress cycloidal disks are CNC-machined from aluminum.
  • Kinematics & Mass Centralization: Drawing inspiration from platforms like Agility Robotics' Digit, the leg kinematics were explicitly designed to operate as a centralized pendulum. By utilizing a dual-stage belt transmission, the heavy ankle actuators are relocated to the mid-thigh. This drastically minimizes distal inertia at the extremities, unlocking the rapid swing-leg retraction speeds necessary for dynamic balancing.
  • Simulation & Control: Trained a robust zero-shot sim-to-real policy in Isaac Lab using PPO, supported by heavily shaped rewards, continuous action smoothing, and extensive domain randomization (variable latency, static/kinetic friction, and mass variation).

Key Achievements

  • Custom High-Torque Actuation: Developed fully integrated, zero-backlash cycloidal actuators capable of sustaining over 40 Nm of peak dynamic torque while providing high-fidelity state estimation via a dual-encoder architecture.
  • Physics-Accurate Simulation: Built an automated pipeline to extract true CAD inertial values and measured physical friction coefficients directly into the URDF, bridging the gap between hardware and the Isaac Lab environment.
  • Hardware Deployment: Conducted hardware testing by deploying the trained ONNX neural network onto the physical biped. These tests utilized a custom distributed impedance control network to execute the policy for brief periods of time, providing critical data for further sim-to-real refinement.
