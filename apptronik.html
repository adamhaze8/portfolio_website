Electrical Engineering Intern at Apptronik

During the summer of 2025, I worked as an Electrical Engineering Intern at Apptronik, a leading company in the rapidly growing humanoid robotics industry. Apptronik specializes in developing next-generation mobile humanoid robots designed to work safely alongside humans in structured environments, pushing the boundaries of generalist robotics. Their flagship humanoid, Apollo, leverages advanced actuation and control systems to handle complex, real-world tasks.

Precise motor control is the foundational hardware control system of any humanoid robot, directly dictating its balance, agility, and ability to safely interact with physical environments. Without optimized low-level motor actuation, higher-level whole-body controllers and learning models cannot reliably execute dynamic movements. I created a comprehensive dynamometer control software architecture to completely automate the process of performance testing both motor hardware and control firmware. This automated system enabled the efficient collection of bulk data covering all relevant motor operating points from long, continuous testing routines. Using these data streams, I conducted detailed motor control studies to characterize electrical behaviors, ultimately identifying the optimal firmware parameters required for high-performance actuation.
